About DeMolay

Origin and Founding

DeMolay International was founded on March 18, 1919, in Kansas City, Missouri, by Frank Sherman Land, a prominent Freemason. The organization was created as a youth fraternity to guide young men in developing strong moral character and leadership skills. The inspiration for DeMolay arose when Land met a young man named Louis Lower, who had recently lost his father. Recognizing the need for mentorship and positive role models in the lives of young men, Land gathered a small group of boys to form a brotherhood based on timeless values such as loyalty, respect, and service. These principles were symbolized by Jacques de Molay, the last Grand Master of the Knights Templar, who embodied fidelity and honor even in the face of persecution.

From its humble beginnings with just nine founding members, DeMolay rapidly grew into an international organization. It became renowned for instilling the principles of responsibility, citizenship, and ethical leadership in its members, all while fostering camaraderie and fellowship. DeMolay has continued to serve as a beacon of moral guidance, encouraging young men to be active and positive influences in their communities. Its rituals, teachings, and traditions remain rooted in the values that inspired its founding, making it one of the most respected youth organizations in the world.

Connection to Freemasonry

DeMolay International has strong ties to Freemasonry, as it was founded by Frank Sherman Land, a dedicated Freemason. Although DeMolay is not a Masonic organization, it was created with Masonic principles at its core, emphasizing values like brotherhood, integrity, and service. Freemasons often serve as advisors and mentors for DeMolay chapters, providing guidance and support to help young men grow into responsible leaders. Many Masonic lodges sponsor DeMolay chapters, offering facilities and resources to ensure their success while fostering a connection between the two organizations.

This relationship also provides a pathway for DeMolay members to explore Freemasonry later in life, as they often develop an appreciation for its principles through their DeMolay experience. Both organizations share a commitment to personal and societal betterment, making their connection a natural alliance. While membership in DeMolay does not require any future commitment to Freemasonry, many DeMolays choose to join Masonic lodges as adults, continuing their journey of self-improvement and service within the Masonic tradition.

Mission

DeMolay International is dedicated to building young men of character and leadership by fostering a commitment to core values such as integrity, loyalty, and service. Through mentorship, education, and a supportive brotherhood, we inspire personal growth, respect for all, and a belief in a higher purpose. DeMolay empowers young men to achieve their full potential, make a positive impact in their communities, and uphold principles that guide them toward lives of purpose and excellence.

Who is Jacque DeMolay

Jacques de Molay (c. 1243â€“1314) was the 23rd and last Grand Master of the Knights Templar, a medieval Christian military order founded during the Crusades to protect pilgrims and holy sites in the Holy Land. Born in France, de Molay joined the Templar Order in his youth and rose to prominence for his dedication and leadership. Under his guidance, the Knights Templar expanded their influence, wealth, and military prowess, becoming one of the most powerful institutions in medieval Europe.

However, by the early 14th century, the Templars faced political and financial pressure. King Philip IV of France, heavily indebted to the Templars, sought to dismantle the order and seize its assets. In 1307, Jacques de Molay and other Templar leaders were arrested on charges of heresy, blasphemy, and corruption, many of which were likely fabricated. After years of imprisonment and torture, de Molay was burned at the stake in 1314 for refusing to confess to false accusations or betray the order. His steadfast loyalty to his beliefs and his brothers in the face of persecution made him a symbol of fidelity, courage, and integrity. Jacques de Molay's legacy inspired the founding of DeMolay International, which upholds his virtues as a model for young men.

Fundamental Freedoms

Religious Freedom

Religious Freedom in DeMolay ensures that every member has the right to follow their own faith or spiritual beliefs without judgment or interference. DeMolay is not affiliated with any specific religion but emphasizes the importance of faith and moral values as cornerstones of personal character. This freedom fosters an environment of tolerance and inclusivity, where members of diverse religious backgrounds can come together in mutual respect. By encouraging members to deepen their own spiritual journey while honoring others' beliefs, DeMolay reinforces the value of unity through diversity.

Civil Freedom

It inspires young men to value the principles of justice, equality, and democracy while actively contributing to their communities. DeMolay encourages civic engagement through service projects, leadership roles, and an understanding of the laws and systems that govern society. By promoting a sense of responsibility and awareness, DeMolay helps members develop the skills and integrity needed to be ethical and effective leaders in their communities

Intellectual Freedom

Intellectual Freedom in DeMolay is the principle that every member has the right and responsibility to think critically, explore new ideas, and form their own opinions. The organization values education and intellectual growth as vital tools for personal development and leadership. Members are encouraged to engage in discussions, ask questions, and challenge themselves to broaden their understanding of the world. This freedom fosters creativity, curiosity, and independent thinking, preparing members to navigate complex challenges and make informed decisions throughout their lives.
